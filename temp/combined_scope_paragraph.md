This study is limited to a pilot implementation within Alaminos City and will not extend to other municipalities or cities in Pangasinan or other provinces. The system will focus on permit processing and inspection tracking but will not automate final approval decisions, which will remain under human authority and discretion of authorized LGU officials. No integration with national government databases including Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR), Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC), Department of Trade and Industry (DTI), or Philippine Identification System (PhilSys) is included due to technical complexity, inter-agency authorization requirements, and data sharing policy constraints. Payment collection will continue through existing LGU payment systems and treasury processes, and historical paper records and previously issued permits processed under the manual system will not be digitized or migrated into the new system—only new applications processed through BizClear from the implementation date forward will be recorded digitally, with historical records continuing to be maintained in their current physical format. Integration with private sector systems such as commercial banks for payment verification, private inspection companies, insurance providers, or other external service providers is not included, and the system will be developed in English and Filipino only, with support for other local languages or dialects not included in this phase.
