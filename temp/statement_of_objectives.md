# Statement of the Problem

The current business permit processing system in Alaminos City faces operational challenges that affect the accuracy and efficiency of business classification and document validation workflows.

# Objectives of the Study

## General Objective
To design and develop BizClear, a blockchain-enhanced business permit processing and inspection tracking system with AI-powered business classification for Alaminos City.

## Specific Objectives

### Objective 1: Problem Analysis and Assessment
**To identify and analyze the specific challenges encountered by BPLO officers in manually encoding business activities and matching tax codes from memory, and assess the misalignment of the current AI document validation system with actual BPLO workflow requirements.**

This objective aims to:
- Document the manual business classification process and tax code mapping challenges
- Analyze the cognitive load on BPLO officers required to memorize A-Z tax code mappings
- Assess the current AI document validation system's misalignment with actual BPLO workflow
- Evaluate the data forwarding process to Manila offices and identify validation gaps

### Objective 2: AI-Powered Business Classification System
**To design and develop an AI-powered business classification system that suggests appropriate tax codes and lines of business to reduce manual encoding errors and cognitive load on BPLO officers.**

This objective will deliver:
- **Automated Tax Code Suggestions**: AI system that analyzes business activity descriptions and suggests appropriate A-Z tax codes
- **Line of Business Classification**: Machine learning model using TF-IDF vectorization to classify business activities accurately
- **Memory Assistance Tool**: Decision support system that reduces the need for officers to memorize tax code mappings
- **Filipino Language Support**: Natural language processing capable of understanding Filipino business descriptions

### Objective 3: Aligned Document Validation System
**To redesign the AI document validation component to align with actual BPLO workflow requirements and data forwarding processes to Manila offices.**

This objective will deliver:
- **Data Quality Validation**: AI system that validates business information consistency rather than ID authenticity
- **Forward-Ready Data Formatting**: System that prepares data for quarterly submission to Manila verification offices
- **Discrepancy Detection**: AI tools that flag potential inconsistencies before data forwarding
- **Audit Trail Integration**: Blockchain-based logging of data validation and forwarding activities
- **Digital Document Management**: Paperless submission and storage system reducing photocopying costs and physical storage requirements

### Objective 4: System Evaluation and Acceptability Assessment
**To evaluate the level of acceptability of the proposed system among BPLO officers and business owners in terms of functionality for accurate task performance, usability for all technical skill levels, and overall efficiency in reducing permit processing time.**

This objective will measure:
- **Functional Performance**: System accuracy in business classification, document processing, and compliance tracking
- **Usability Metrics**: User satisfaction across different technical skill levels and role-specific requirements
- **Efficiency Improvements**: Quantifiable reductions in processing time, office visits, and document redundancy
- **Compliance Standards**: Adherence to Republic Act 11032 processing time requirements (7 working days for complex transactions)
- **User Acceptance**: Adoption rates and satisfaction scores from both government officers and business owners
- **System Reliability**: Performance metrics including uptime, response times, and error rates

## Expected Outcomes and Performance Targets

### Classification Accuracy Improvement
- Achieve 95% accuracy in AI-powered business classification to reduce manual encoding errors
- Improve classification consistency and reduce cognitive load on BPLO officers
- Support Filipino language input natively for local business descriptions

### Processing Time Reduction
- Reduce permit processing from current 3-4 weeks to within RA 11032's 7-working-day standard
- Minimize multiple office visits from 5 separate visits to single digital submission
- Eliminate redundant document submissions through centralized digital storage

### Compliance and Security Enhancement
- Ensure 100% compliance tracking for post-requirement monitoring within 30-working-day mandates
- Provide tamper-proof audit trails through blockchain cryptographic hashing
- Enable real-time compliance dashboards for LGU administrators

### User Experience Improvement
- Achieve 90%+ user satisfaction rates among both BPLO officers and business owners
- Provide intuitive interfaces accessible to users with varying technical skill levels
- Enable 24/7 access to application status and historical compliance records

## Success Criteria

The BizClear system will be considered successful when it demonstrates:
1. **Problem Resolution**: Addresses all identified manual workflow inefficiencies
2. **Performance Standards**: Meets or exceeds RA 11032 processing time requirements
3. **User Acceptance**: Achieves high satisfaction and adoption rates among target users
4. **Technical Excellence**: Provides secure, reliable, and scalable digital infrastructure
5. **Regulatory Compliance**: Maintains data integrity and audit trail requirements for government operations

These objectives align with the Input-Process-Output framework established in the study, ensuring systematic development and evaluation of a comprehensive digital solution for Alaminos City's business permit processing challenges.
